PREFERRED BIDDER SELECTED FOR TWICKENHAM RIVERSIDE
----------------------------------------------------------------------

    Following a 2-stage competition process and extensive public
    engagement, Richmond Council and the Royal Institute of British
    Architects are delighted to announce that Hopkins Architects have
    been selected as the preferred bidder.

       https://stmargarets.london/archives/2019/11/lbrut-twick-riverside-bidder-selected.html?utm_source=newsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=traffic%2Bfrom%20text%20newsletter



RICHMOND COMMITS TO REAL LIVING WAGE
----------------------------------------------------------------------

    Richmond Council has this week been accredited as a Living
    Wage Employer.

    The Living Wage commitment recognises that everyone working
    at Richmond Council receives a minimum hourly wage of 10.75.
    This rate is significantly higher than the Government minimum
    for over 25s, which currently stands at 8.21 per hour.

    o  read more on the website
       https://stmargarets.london/archives/2019/11/lbrut-living-wage.html?utm_source=newsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=traffic%2Bfrom%20text%20newsletter



IMPLEMENTATION OF NEW DIY DISPOSAL CHARGE DELAYED FOR REVIEW
----------------------------------------------------------------------

    A new charge for residents wanting to dispose of construction and
    demolition waste ("DIY waste") at Richmond's Household Waste and
    Recycling Centre in Kew will be delayed, to enable a review of how
    it will be implemented.

    o  read more on the website
       https://stmargarets.london/archives/2019/11/lbrut-diy-waste-charging-delayed.html?utm_source=newsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=traffic%2Bfrom%20text%20newsletter



BANK YOUR BRAS AT RICHMOND COUNCIL
----------------------------------------------------------------------

    Calling all ladies of Richmond upon Thames - we need your old bras!

    Richmond Council has teamed up with Against Breast Cancer to
    collect old bras - for every tonne of bras collected, the charity
    receives vital funds for pioneering breast cancer research.

    o  read more on the website
       https://stmargarets.london/archives/2019/11/lbrut-bra-donations.html?utm_source=newsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=traffic%2Bfrom%20text%20newsletter
